How This Calculator Works

This calculator converts your annual gross salary into hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly rates — both gross (before tax) and net (after Income Tax).

Gross Rate Calculation

Your gross hourly rate is calculated as:

1

Annual ÷ Weeks ÷ Hours

Hourly = Annual Salary ÷ Weeks per Year ÷ Hours per Week

Example: £30,000 ÷ 52 ÷ 37.5 = £15.38/hour

Net Rate Calculation

Net pay is calculated by applying the UK Income Tax bands to your annual salary, then dividing the net annual figure by weeks and hours:

1

Deduct Personal Allowance

Taxable income = Gross salary − £12,570 (Personal Allowance)

2

Apply Progressive Tax Bands

Each portion of income is taxed at the applicable rate (20%, 40%, or 45%)

3

Calculate Net Annual

Net Annual = Gross − Total Income Tax

4

Divide to Get Hourly/Daily/Weekly

Net Hourly = Net Annual ÷ Weeks ÷ Hours

Assumptions & Limitations

Income Tax only
This calculator shows Income Tax deductions only. National Insurance, pension contributions, student loan repayments, and other deductions are not included.
Standard Personal Allowance
Assumes the standard Personal Allowance of £12,570. If your income exceeds £100,000, your allowance is reduced. If you have a different tax code, your results will differ.
Employment income only
Assumes all income is from employment. Dividend income, rental income, savings interest, and other income types are taxed differently.
2025/26 tax year
Uses HMRC tax bands for the 2025/26 tax year (6 April 2025 to 5 April 2026). Rates may change in future budgets.
Scotland option
The Scotland option applies Scottish Income Tax rates. Scottish taxpayers still pay UK National Insurance rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my hourly rate from my annual salary?

Divide your annual salary by the number of weeks you work per year, then divide by your weekly hours. For a standard 37.5-hour week over 52 weeks: Annual ÷ 52 ÷ 37.5. For a 40-hour week: Annual ÷ 52 ÷ 40.

Why is my net pay different from what I actually receive?

This calculator shows Income Tax only. Your actual take-home pay will also be reduced by National Insurance (8% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270), pension contributions, student loan repayments, and any other deductions your employer makes.

What is the effective tax rate?

The effective tax rate is the percentage of your total gross income paid as Income Tax. It is always lower than your marginal rate (the rate on your highest band of income) because the lower bands are taxed at lower rates. For example, a £50,000 salary has a marginal rate of 40% but an effective Income Tax rate of around 18%.

Does this calculator include the 2025/26 tax bands?

Yes. The calculator uses the official HMRC Income Tax bands for 2025/26: Personal Allowance £12,570, Basic Rate 20% (up to £50,270), Higher Rate 40% (up to £125,140), and Additional Rate 45% above £125,140. Scotland rates are also included.
