How EU Income Tax Works

While there is no single EU-wide income tax, most EU member states share common features in how they tax employment income:

📈
Progressive Tax Bands
Most EU countries use progressive income tax — higher earners pay a higher marginal rate on the portion of income above each threshold.
🏥
Social Contributions
Employees pay mandatory contributions to pension, health insurance, and unemployment funds. Rates vary significantly by country.
💼
Employer Withholding
In most EU countries, employers withhold income tax and social contributions from your gross salary each month before paying you.
📋
Annual Tax Return
Many EU countries require an annual tax return to reconcile withholding with actual tax owed, especially for complex situations.
