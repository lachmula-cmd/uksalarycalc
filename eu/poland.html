Poland PIT Income Tax Bands 2024

Poland's income tax (PIT — Podatek dochodowy od osób fizycznych) uses a two-rate progressive system with a significant tax-free amount (kwota wolna od podatku). The 2022 "Polski Ład" reform raised the tax-free amount to 30,000 PLN and reduced the standard rate to 12%.

Band Rate Taxable Income Range Notes
Tax-free amount 0% Up to 30,000 PLN Kwota wolna od podatku — applies to all taxpayers
Standard rate 12% 30,001 – 120,000 PLN Reduced from 17% in 2022 under Polski Ład
Higher rate 32% Above 120,000 PLN Applies to taxable income above the threshold
PIT base: PIT is calculated on gross income minus ZUS contributions (pension, disability, sickness). The tax-free amount of 30,000 PLN means the first 30,000 PLN of taxable income is effectively untaxed.

ZUS — Social Insurance Contributions (Employee)

ZUS (Zakład Ubezpieczeń Społecznych) is Poland's social insurance institution. Employees on umowa o pracę pay the following contributions, deducted from gross salary:

Pension (Emerytalne)
Funds the state pension (ZUS) and/or OFE (open pension fund). Split equally between employee and employer.
9.76%
Disability (Rentowe)
Funds disability pension benefits. Employee pays 1.5%, employer pays 6.5%.
1.5%
Sickness (Chorobowe)
Funds sick pay and maternity benefits. Paid entirely by the employee.
2.45%
Health Insurance (NFZ)
Funds the National Health Fund (Narodowy Fundusz Zdrowia). Calculated on gross minus ZUS contributions. Not deductible from PIT since 2022.
9%
Total employee ZUS: 13.71% of gross salary (pension 9.76% + disability 1.5% + sickness 2.45%). Health insurance (9%) is calculated separately on gross minus ZUS. Employer also pays ZUS contributions of approximately 20% on top of your gross salary.

Key Facts About the Polish Tax System

12% / 32% PIT Rates
30,000 zł Tax-Free Amount
13.71% Employee ZUS
9% Health Insurance
  • Poland's tax year runs from 1 January to 31 December.
  • The 2022 "Polski Ład" reform raised the tax-free amount from 8,000 PLN to 30,000 PLN and cut the standard rate from 17% to 12%.
  • B2B contractors (działalność gospodarcza) can choose between different tax regimes: general PIT, flat 19% tax (podatek liniowy), or lump-sum (ryczałt).
  • PPK (Pracownicze Plany Kapitałowe) is a voluntary workplace pension scheme — employees contribute 2% (default), employers add 1.5%.
  • Poland has a relatively low minimum wage compared to Western EU countries, but wages have been rising rapidly in recent years.
  • The employer pays approximately 20% of your gross salary in additional ZUS contributions (accident, labour fund, FGŚP).
  • Annual PIT returns (PIT-37 or PIT-36) are due by 30 April of the following year, though most employees have tax settled automatically by their employer.

Frequently Asked Questions — Poland Tax

What is the difference between umowa o pracę and B2B in Poland?

Umowa o pracę (employment contract) provides full employee protections, paid leave, and employer ZUS contributions. B2B (business-to-business) means you operate as a sole trader — you pay your own ZUS contributions but can choose a more favourable tax regime (e.g., 19% flat tax). B2B typically results in higher net pay but fewer protections.

What is Polski Ład and how did it change taxes?

Polski Ład (Polish Deal) was a major tax reform introduced in 2022. Key changes included: raising the tax-free amount to 30,000 PLN, reducing the standard PIT rate from 17% to 12%, and removing the deductibility of health insurance contributions from PIT. The reform benefited lower and middle earners but increased the burden on some higher earners.

Why is my net salary lower than expected?

In Poland, the health insurance contribution (9%) is no longer deductible from PIT since the 2022 reform. This means you pay both PIT and health insurance on the same income, increasing the effective deduction rate. Combined with ZUS contributions, total deductions for a typical employee can reach 30–35% of gross salary.

What is PPK?

PPK (Pracownicze Plany Kapitałowe) is Poland's workplace pension scheme. Employees automatically contribute 2% of gross salary (can be reduced to 0.5% for lower earners), employers add 1.5%, and the state adds a one-off 250 PLN welcome bonus plus 240 PLN annually. You can opt out, but opting in builds long-term retirement savings.

How does Poland compare to Germany or the UK for take-home pay?

Poland has significantly lower average gross salaries than Germany or the UK, but also a lower cost of living — particularly outside Warsaw. The effective tax rate for a 120,000 PLN salary is around 28–30%, which is comparable to many Western European countries. See our guide on why net pay differs by country.
