Key Differences Between Global Tax Systems

🇺🇸
USA: State Taxes Matter
Federal tax is just one layer. State income taxes range from 0% (Texas, Florida) to 13.3% (California). Your actual take-home depends heavily on which state you live in.
🇨🇦
Canada: Provincial Taxes Add Up
Federal tax is combined with provincial tax. Ontario's top combined rate is ~53.5%. Alberta is lower at ~48%. Provincial rates significantly affect take-home pay.
🇦🇺
Australia: Super is Employer-Paid
Australia's Superannuation (11.5%) is paid by the employer on top of your gross salary — it is not deducted from your pay. This makes Australian gross-to-net comparisons simpler than other countries.
🇨🇭
Switzerland: Cantonal Taxes Vary Widely
Switzerland has one of the lowest federal income tax rates in Europe (max 11.5%), but cantonal and municipal taxes can add 10–25% more. Zug and Schwyz are low-tax cantons; Geneva and Vaud are higher.
🇳🇴
Norway: High Tax, High Services
Norway combines a flat 22% income tax with a progressive step tax (trinnskatt) and a 7.9% social contribution. High taxes fund universal healthcare, education, and generous social benefits.
🇦🇪
UAE: Zero Income Tax for Expats
The UAE has no personal income tax for expatriates. Your gross salary is your net salary. This makes the UAE highly attractive for high earners, though cost of living in Dubai and Abu Dhabi can be significant.
