Quick Concepts

New to salary and tax terminology? Here are the key terms explained in one sentence each.

Gross Salary
Your total pay before any deductions — the number on your employment contract.
Net Salary
Your take-home pay after income tax, social contributions, and other deductions.
Income Tax
A progressive tax on your earnings, paid to the government to fund public services.
Social Contributions
Mandatory contributions that fund specific benefits — pensions, healthcare, unemployment insurance.
Personal Allowance
The amount of income you can earn before paying income tax (e.g., £12,570 in the UK).
Marginal Rate
The tax rate on your highest band of income — not the rate on all your income.
Effective Rate
The average tax rate across all your income — always lower than your marginal rate.
Tax Residency
The country where you are considered a tax resident — determines which country's tax rules apply to you.
