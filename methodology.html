Methodology & Data Sources

How our calculators work, where our data comes from, and what assumptions we make. Transparency is important to us — this page explains exactly how we produce our estimates.

Transparency commitment: We believe users deserve to understand how our calculators work. This page documents our methodology, data sources, and limitations in full. All results are estimates — we never claim otherwise.

1. General Approach

SalaryUKCalc.com uses a client-side calculation model. All calculations run entirely in your browser using JavaScript. No salary data is sent to our servers — we never see, store, or process your financial information.

Our calculators use a simplified progressive tax model. We apply published tax bands and rates to a gross annual salary figure, deduct applicable allowances, and calculate estimated income tax and social contributions. The result is an estimated net annual salary.

We distinguish between two tiers of calculator:

Tier 1: UK Detailed Calculator
Our UK salary calculator uses official HMRC tax bands for the current tax year (2025/26). It applies the standard Personal Allowance and progressive Income Tax bands for both England/Wales/Northern Ireland and Scotland. This is our most accurate calculator. It calculates Income Tax only — National Insurance and other deductions are not included but are clearly noted.
Tier 2: EU & Global Estimators
Our EU and Global country calculators are simplified estimators. They use publicly available national/federal tax rates and standard deductions to produce a gross-to-net estimate. These are clearly labelled as estimates throughout the site. They do not include state/provincial taxes, personal deductions beyond standard allowances, or other individual circumstances.

2. UK Calculator Methodology

Data Source

UK Income Tax bands and rates are sourced from HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). Scotland rates are sourced from the Scottish Government.

Calculation Steps

1

Input: Gross Annual Salary

User enters gross annual salary, hours per week, weeks per year, and days per week.

2

Apply Personal Allowance

Taxable income = Gross salary − Personal Allowance (£12,570 for 2025/26). If gross exceeds £100,000, the Personal Allowance is tapered at £1 for every £2 above £100,000, reaching £0 at £125,140.

3

Apply Progressive Tax Bands

Each portion of taxable income is taxed at the applicable band rate. For England/Wales: 20% (Basic), 40% (Higher), 45% (Additional). For Scotland: 19% (Starter), 20% (Basic), 21% (Intermediate), 42% (Higher), 45% (Advanced), 48% (Top).

4

Calculate Net Annual

Net Annual = Gross − Total Income Tax. Hourly/daily/weekly/monthly rates are derived by dividing net annual by the appropriate periods.

What Is Not Included

  • National Insurance contributions
  • Pension contributions (auto-enrolment or otherwise)
  • Student loan repayments
  • Salary sacrifice deductions
  • Benefits in kind
  • Tax code adjustments beyond the standard Personal Allowance

5. What Our Estimators Do Not Include

The following are not included in any of our EU or Global estimators:

  • State / provincial / regional / local taxes — US state income taxes, Canadian provincial taxes, German church tax (Kirchensteuer), Spanish regional IRPF surcharges, etc.
  • Filing status variations — married filing jointly, dependants, household composition
  • Personal deductions beyond standard allowances — mortgage interest, charitable donations, business expenses, etc.
  • Tax credits — child tax credits, earned income credits, etc.
  • Employer-specific arrangements — salary sacrifice, company cars, private health insurance
  • Self-employment income — all calculators assume standard employment income
  • Investment income — dividends, capital gains, rental income

7. Technical Implementation

All calculations are implemented in vanilla JavaScript (no frameworks or external libraries). The core calculation logic is contained in data/countries.js, which stores tax band data and the estimateNetSalary() function. The UK calculator logic is in script.js.

Key technical characteristics:

  • All calculations run client-side in the browser
  • No data is sent to any server
  • No cookies are set by the calculators themselves
  • Results update in real time as inputs change
  • Currency formatting uses the browser's built-in Intl.NumberFormat API

8. Reporting Errors

If you believe any tax rate, threshold, or calculation on this site is incorrect, please contact us with details. We take accuracy seriously and will investigate and correct any errors promptly.

Please include: the country/calculator, the input values you used, the result you received, and what you believe the correct result should be (with a source reference if possible).
